About Me

Hi,

My name is Marcin Kowalczyk and I’m a UI · UX designer.

In my projects, I always want to go out of the box and get off the beaten paths of mediocrity.

I'm looking for the new possibilities and challenges in design. It doesn't matter what kind of project it is. Websites, software interfaces or application screens. Every case is different and creates a space for our imagination.

Each project is independent and one of its kind. We can dress the concepts of digital aesthetics in sophisticated forms of unified style patterns like “flat design” or set up flagship canons under the charming name of “material design”, but every project should be unique and giving best experiences for users. That’s why I love to use animation in my projects. I can breath life into the static form and give them more expressive and individuality look.

That is how I think and work. Enigmatic?

Maybe a little :)

I think my designs speak more than words. So, check out my works and I hope you enjoy it.

Cheers.

The world-famous festival / concept

Oktoberfest

/ webpage redesign

# 99design.com contest entry

# year: 2016



Keynotes from the brief:
‍
“The website should be high glossy, inspirational, emotional and useful. The color scheme could be blue/white.”


Content details:
‍
Homepage:
“The homepage welcomes the user with an emotional image from the Oktoberfest and guides him to the different sections of the page. Also, it contains current information.”

Detail page of POI (for example a tent):
“Is a description landing page of a POI including a big image or maybe a video. It contains also a minimap.”

Main page

The designed really nice and easy to use the home page. I used a blue as the main color theme and a lot of white space for the clean look. I put all of the most important information in the second and the third section (accommodation). In that kind of such a big event is really important to show it on the main page in a visible place. Others section are pretty typical: news, Shop, Gallery and the footer.

Detail page

The style is similar to a home page. I used a Google Page Map in the right column. In my opinion, could be really important for the localization of the tents. It's helpful to click on the map in your mobile phone and find a direct route by only one tap. Especially moving with such a big crowd like on this kind of event.
